CSS Course Lesson 3

Fonts and Formatting

Fonts are the different styles or designs of text that appear on a webpage. Just like handwriting styles, fonts change the way letters and words look. Fonts can feel professional, fun, serious, or creative—depending on their design.

Click to reveal these fonts!

The font shorthand property in CSS is used to define all the font-related settings of an element in one line. It can also be used to apply a system-defined font to the element.

Interactive Activity: Try Your Own Fonts!

Replace "Arial" with any font that’s installed on your computer—for example, Verdana, Times New Roman, Comic Sans MS, or another font you like!

In this game, your goal is to match text with its correct font style. This will help you become familiar with the different ways text can be styled in web design. Just drag and drop to arrange the code blocks on the right to match the output on the left!
